Trello loves showing off the tools developers have built on the Trello Platform. There are two types of applications we show-case, Power-Ups and Integrations. To have your app added to our listings, follow the steps below.

Power-Ups vs. Integrations

Unsure of the difference between Power-Ups and Integrations? Power-Ups make use of the the Power-Up API and platform. They are given canvas realestate on the core Trello product. Most Power-Ups will also make use of Trello's RESTful API, but it isn't a requirement. Power-Ups are able to be enabled on boards by Trello users without having to leave Trello. Integrations are often add-ons within other apps and tools that interact with Trello via the RESTful API. Integrations also include Chrome extensions.

Power-Up Specific Details

Our bar for 3rd party Power-Ups is that they should provide a user experience at the level of Trello itself. They should not do too much, or too little, and should generally be free of bugs and general UX awkwardness.

Check out our Brand Guidelines. Power-Ups should be an extension of the Trello experience. We want Trello users to love using your Power-Up as much as they love using Trello! You can also check out our brand assets.

Power-Up Requirements
  • Power-Up must provide a way to disassociate user authorization via the show-settings capability.
  • There should be no JS errors in console.
Power-Ups Hosting

Power-Ups should be hosted by the development team who built the Power-Ups.
